Who are we?

We are Ireland’s largest surf club that consists of mostly beginners, as well as experienced surfers. We come together to celebrate the sport of surfing and the warm and fun culture that surfing promotes. The objective of the club is to get our members surfing as much as possible through a friendly and safe environment. We organise numerous trips to the west coast of Ireland as well as trips abroad.
We also highlight the importance of water safety and offer our members the opportunity to complete the NBLQ Beach Lifeguard course. We allow beginners and experienced surfers to compete in the Surf Intervarsity Championship and Colours. We show our members that surfing is a fun, challenging sport and a great way to meet new people.

Do I need to know how to surf to join?

Not at all! The majority of our members are at beginner level. Even if you have never touched a surfboard before you will find this club will be the perfect way to help you catch your first wave and introduce you to many friends along the way.
The club is as well tailored to the experienced surfers who will gain huge amounts from the culture and environment the club promotes. If you’re looking for a way to get out on your board more, this club is for you.

What does my membership include?

Besides the memories of a lifetime you will be eligible to participate in the following:
- Our surf trips to the west coast. Trips are organized every three weeks. All attending will have accommodation, rentals, and lessons organized through the club.
- Inter-university surf competitions such as Intervarsities and Colours.
- Our weekly surf yoga training sessions in the team gym.
- Other club organized events such as the black tie Surf Ball, our nights out, and our humanitarian events such as last year’s charity Take Me Out.
- Our NBLQ Beach Lifeguard course at discounted price.

OUR TRIPS

Surf the Wild Atlantic Way

Bundoran

This is our first trip of the year with over 70 people. For many this is the first adventure where surf club steals their heart and never gives it back. This trip includes massive 12 bed houses beside the sea, lessons with Turn'N'Surf, and a traditional Black Tie night to the exclusive Paris nightclub. We revisit Bundoran on our daytrips, and again for our first trip of semester 2.

ENNISCRONE

Our legendary three night Halloween surf trip. This is not one to miss surfers. In the quaint and beautiful town of Enniscrone you will be placed in a warm and comfortable 8-person house and given three days to run around, surf some of the best waves in Ireland, and dress up as terrifyingly or creatively as you can for our Halloween Surf Party. The houses are walking distance to the beach and surf school, there’s plenty of shops and pubs to explore, and we even throw in a massive gum-ball challenge where every house will duke it out for surf club glory.

FOREIGN TRIP

UCD Surf Club was the first surf club in Ireland to organize a large scale college trip to Morocco. Each year has been more and more of a success. Surf Club works hard in organizing our foreign trip so that our members can become immersed in a foreign culture, experience breathtaking settings, and surf some of the best waves in the world. This year Surf Club are organizing a semester one trip to Surf Snowdonia in Wales. There we will compete in Intervarsities at the world’s first ever artificial surfing wave pool. Our second semester trip is currently being planned. This year we are taking a break from Morocco to allow our members to experience the waves of Portugal

DUNFANAGHY

Last year was UCD Surf’s first time in the quiet and peaceful town of Dunfanaghy. It was love at first sight. The town is a brilliant location for small surf trips with it’s incredibly developed and advanced Narosa Surf School, amazing hikes through local forests, mountains, and Glenveagh National Park; as well as some of the most comfortable and beautiful houses UCD Surf has ever stayed in. A perfect recipe for a fun and memorable surf trip.

Beginner's FAQ

  • Do I need my own board and wetsuit?

    No, your own equipment is not necessary. There will be the option on every surf trip to pay for rentals.

  • How much does a trip cost?

    A weekend away trip will usually cost around 80 euro if you don’t have your own equipment or your own transportation. It can be broken down into 30 euro for accommodation, 20 euro for weekend rentals, and 30 euro for transportation there and back.

  • How often are the trips?

    Trips are every three weeks. Three of these trips per semester will be considered the club’s major trips – the ones with usually 40-80 people. Joining Surf Club will also allow you to be acquainted with our club’s surfers who go to catch waves every weekend.

  • Where do we go?

    Our trips are mainly to the west coast with our favourite spots being Bundoran, Enniscrone and Lahinch. The club is always experimenting with new surf spots.

  • How do I join?

    Membership fee is 15 euro which can be paid at the surf fresher’s stand during freshers week or after fresher’s week by contacting surf@ucd.ie.

  • How do you know there will be waves on the trip?

    To be honest, we don’t. Only Poseidon can guarantee you waves. That is why we often travel to the same places, because they consistently provide the most reliable surf.

  • No waves?

    If we do get unlucky and the sea is flat, the club organizes many other fun outdoor activities such as stand up paddle boarding, hiking, and sight seeing. There will always be plenty to do on a surf trip.

  • Can I choose to stay in a house with my friends?

    Of course we will do the best we can to put you with your mates, but part of the culture of the club is to embrace meeting new people and making new friends and we hope our members embrace it.

  • I have another question

    Drop an email to us: surf@ucd.ie
